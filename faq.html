Frequently Asked Questions

Clarity for your creative journey. Find answers to common admissions, portfolio, and campus life questions.

What is required for the portfolio submission?+
Your portfolio should confidently represent your unique creative voice. We require 12-15 pieces of recent, original work. This can span across mediums—whether sketches, digital art, video essays, or coded interactive projects. Emphasize quality and conceptual depth over quantity.
Are international students eligible for financial aid?+
Absolutely. We believe that global perspectives vastly enrich our creative environment. International applicants are automatically considered for the "Global Visionary Merit Scholarship" upon admission. Need-based aid forms can also be downloaded from our forms page.
How does the PhD Program in Creativity work?+
As the nation's first PhD in Creativity, this is a highly selective, interdisciplinary, research-driven program. It bridges cognitive science, artistic practice, and leadership. Candidates spend 3-4 years creating a master thesis that redefines how creativity interfaces with society, technology, or business.
Is on-campus housing guaranteed for freshmen?+
Yes, all first-year undergraduate students are guaranteed placement in our state-of-the-art "Atelier Residences," which feature built-in studio spaces, collaborative lounges, and 24/7 access to high-end digital labs right in the building.
